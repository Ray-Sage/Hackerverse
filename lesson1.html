Lesson 1: Understanding Phishing Attacks

üìå What is Phishing?

Phishing is one of the most common cyber threats where attackers disguise themselves as trusted organizations (like banks, delivery companies, or even government agencies) to trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or social security details.

Real-Life Example:
In 2020, cybercriminals sent millions of emails pretending to be from Netflix. The message claimed the user's subscription was expiring and asked them to "update payment details." Victims who clicked the link were taken to a fake website that stole their credit card numbers.

üîç How to Spot a Phishing Attempt

  • Unexpected requests for personal information.
  • Suspicious email addresses (e.g., "support@netf1ix.com").
  • Urgent or threatening language ("Your account will be closed today!").
  • Links that redirect to fake websites.
  • Poor spelling and grammar in messages.
