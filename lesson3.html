Lesson 3: Malware & Ransomware

üíª What is Malware?

Malware (malicious software) is any program or file designed to harm, exploit, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system. Malware includes viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, adware, and ransomware.

Real-Life Example:
In 2017, the WannaCry ransomware attack infected over 200,000 computers across 150 countries. It locked files on Windows computers and demanded Bitcoin payments to restore access. Hospitals, banks, and businesses were severely disrupted, showing the devastating impact of ransomware.

üîé Common Types of Malware

  • Virus: Attaches to files and spreads when those files are shared.
  • Worm: Self-replicates across networks without user action.
  • Trojan: Disguises as legitimate software to trick users.
  • Spyware: Secretly monitors user activity (like keyloggers).
  • Adware: Shows unwanted ads and can slow systems down.
  • Ransomware: Locks or encrypts files until a ransom is paid.
