Passwords are the first line of defense for your online accounts. Weak or reused passwords are one of the top reasons hackers gain access to sensitive data. According to Verizon‚Äôs Data Breach Report (2023), over 80% of breaches involved stolen or weak passwords.

Real-Life Example:
In 2012, LinkedIn suffered a massive data breach where over 117 million user accounts were leaked. Many passwords were simple like "123456" or "password," making it easy for attackers to exploit other accounts since users often reuse the same password across different sites.

üõ†Ô∏è Characteristics of a Strong Password

  • At least 12 characters long.
  • Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special symbols.
  • Not based on dictionary words or personal details (birthdays, names).
  • Unique for every account (no reuse!).
Pro Tip: Use a password manager like Bitwarden, 1Password, or LastPass to generate and store strong, unique passwords for every account. This way, you only need to remember one master password.

üõ°Ô∏è Additional Protection: 2-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Even the strongest password can be compromised. 2FA adds an extra step, like a text code, authenticator app, or biometric verification, making it far harder for attackers to break in.
